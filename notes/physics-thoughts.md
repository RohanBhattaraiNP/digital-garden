# Random Physics Thoughts

Not organized. Just things that blow my mind.

## The Speed of Light Isn't About Light

$c$ isn't the speed of light. It's the speed of **causality**. The maximum speed at which information can propagate through spacetime.

Light just happens to travel at this speed because photons have zero mass.

## We're All Time Travelers

You're moving through time at exactly 1 second per second. But you can change that ratio by moving fast or being near massive objects.

Time dilation isn't sci-fi. GPS satellites experience it. Without relativistic corrections, your GPS would be off by kilometers.

## Quantum Mechanics is Weirder Than People Think

People say "observation collapses the wavefunction" and think it means consciousness matters.

No. "Observation" means "interaction." Anything that interacts with a quantum system (detector, photon, air molecule) causes decoherence.

The universe doesn't care if you're watching. It cares if anything is touching anything else.

## The Uncertainty Principle Isn't About Measurement Errors

$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

This isn't "we can't measure precisely." It's "particles don't HAVE precise position and momentum simultaneously."

The universe is fundamentally fuzzy at small scales. Not because of our tools - because that's how reality works.

## Entropy Always Increases

$$\Delta S \geq 0$$

This is the only physics law with a direction of time. Everything else works forwards and backwards.

Why does time only move forward? Because entropy increases. That's it.

## Black Holes Are Simple

Only three properties: mass, charge, angular momentum. That's it.

Doesn't matter if you make it from hydrogen or old computers or pizza. All black holes with same M, Q, J are identical.

Information paradox still unsolved though. Where does the information go?

## Quantum Entanglement Can't Send Information

Sorry, no FTL communication. Entanglement is real, but you can't use it to transmit data.

Measuring one particle instantly affects the other, but the measurement results are random. You only see the correlation after comparing notes classically (at light speed).

Universe's way of saying: "I'll break locality but I won't break causality."
