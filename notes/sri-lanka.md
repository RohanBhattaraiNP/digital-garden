# Sri Lanka: The Best Country I've Been To

Controversial take? Maybe. But hear me out.

## Why Sri Lanka Wins

### 1. Diversity in a Small Package

You can surf on the south coast in the morning, be in tea plantations in the central highlands by afternoon, and watch elephants in the evening. All in one day.

The entire country is roughly the size of West Virginia but packs more variety than continents.

### 2. The People

Warmest people I've ever met. Period. Not "tourist warm" - genuinely, authentically kind.

Got lost in Kandy trying to find a temple. Random uncle on a motorbike stopped, told me to hop on, drove me there, refused payment, and invited me for tea at his house.

That's not an exception. That's the norm.

### 3. The Train Rides

![Train through Sri Lankan highlands](PLACEHOLDER_IMAGE)

The Kandy to Ella train journey might be the most beautiful train ride in the world. Hanging out of the door, tea plantations rolling by, mist in the mountains.

No phone signal. Just... present.

### 4. Food That Ruins You

After eating rice and curry in Sri Lanka (the real stuff, not tourist versions), every other rice dish feels like a rough draft.

The spice complexity, the coconut, the freshness. My taste buds have never been the same.

### 5. Affordable Paradise

You can live like royalty for $30 a day. But that's not even the point - it's just a bonus.

## What Makes It #1

Other countries have better beaches (Maldives). Better mountains (Nepal). Better temples (Cambodia).

But **no country has better balance.** Nature, culture, people, food, adventure, relaxation - all in perfect equilibrium.

And unlike many tourist-heavy places, Sri Lanka still feels... real. Like people are living their lives, and you just get to respectfully observe.

## Practical Notes

- Best time: December to March (dry season on west/south coasts)
- Skip Colombo, head straight to Galle
- Take the trains, not buses
- Eat at local spots, not hotels
- Learn a few Tamil/Sinhala phrases - locals love it

Sri Lanka isn't perfect (nowhere is). But it's the place I think about most when I can't sleep. The place I'd go back to in a heartbeat.

The place that reset my baseline for what travel can be.
