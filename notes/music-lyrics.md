# Music I Listen To & Lines That Stick

Music taste is personal. These are mine.

## Hip Hop / Rap

### Kendrick Lamar

*"I remember you was conflicted, misusing your influence."*
— The Art of Peer Pressure

The way he captures internal conflict? Unmatched.

*"We gon' be alright."*
— Alright

Sometimes you just need to hear this.

### J. Cole

*"Life is a balance, you lose your grip, you can slip into an abyss, no doubt you see these niggas trippin'. Ego in charge of every move, he's a star, and we can't look away."*
— Immortal

Ego death before physical death.

## Alternative / Indie

### Radiohead

*"For a minute there, I lost myself."*
— Karma Police

One line. Entire experience captured.

### Bon Iver

*"And at once I knew I was not magnificent."*
— Holocene

Cosmic insignificance as comfort.

## Lo-Fi / Study Beats

Not about lyrics. About focus. About flow state.

Jinsang, Idealism, SwuM. When I need to disappear into work.

## Indian Classical

Raag Bhimpalasi in the evening. Raag Yaman at night.

No lyrics needed. Just... space.

## What I'm Looking For in Music

### 1. Emotional Precision

Vague feelings made specific. That's poetry.

### 2. Sonic Texture

Not just what's said, but how it sounds. Production matters.

### 3. Replayability

If I can only listen once, it's not good enough. Best songs get better with repetition.

## Lines That Changed How I Think

*"The wound is the place where the Light enters you."*
— Rumi (not music, but often sampled)

*"What dies when I die?"*

If the answer is just "me," you haven't built anything lasting.

## Music vs Silence

I used to need constant sound. Now I appreciate silence.

Music enhances moments. It shouldn't replace them.
